Electrician Terms Reference

Industry glossary with 50 terms across multiple skill levels

Showing 50 of 50 terms

Circuit breaker

ID: 52 Level 1

A device that stops the flow of electricity when there is a fault.

Kilowatt (kW)

ID: 156 Level 1

A unit of power equal to 1,000 watts.

Knockout Panel

ID: 159 Level 2

A removable panel in equipment or walls to allow access or wiring.

Voltage

ID: 290 Level 2

The electrical force that makes current flow in a circuit.

Pliers and Side Cutter

ID: 365 Level 1

Tools used to grip, bend, or cut wire and other materials.

Conduit Bender

ID: 571 Level 1

A tool used to bend electrical conduit to specific angles for routing.

Fish Tape

ID: 572 Level 1

A tool used to route new wiring through walls and electrical conduit.

Wire Stripper

ID: 573 Level 1

A tool designed to remove insulation from electrical wires without damaging the conductor.

Junction Box

ID: 574 Level 1

An enclosure that protects electrical connections and provides access for maintenance.

Busbar

ID: 575 Level 3

A metallic strip or bar that conducts electricity within a switchboard or panel.

Load Center

ID: 576 Level 1

A service panel that distributes electrical power to various circuits in a building.

Electrical Rough-In

ID: 577 Level 1

The first stage of electrical installation including wiring and boxes before walls are closed.

Ampacity

ID: 578 Level 3

The maximum amount of electric current a conductor can carry before sustaining damage.

Wire Nut

ID: 579 Level 1

A connector used to fasten two or more electrical conductors together.

Contactor

ID: 580 Level 3

An electrically controlled switch used for switching power circuits, often for motors.

Overload Relay

ID: 581 Level 3

A protective device that opens a circuit when current exceeds a preset value for a period of time.

Conduit Fill

ID: 582 Level 3

The percentage of conduit space occupied by conductors as limited by electrical codes.

Panelboard

ID: 583 Level 2

A component of an electrical distribution system that divides power into subsidiary circuits.

Ballast

ID: 584 Level 2

A device that regulates current flow and provides starting voltage for discharge lamps.

Phase Sequence

ID: 587 Level 3

The order in which the voltages in a three-phase system reach their peak values.

Soft Starter

ID: 592 Level 3

A device that reduces voltage during motor startup to limit inrush current and mechanical stress.

High Voltage Switchgear

ID: 593 Level 3

Electrical equipment used to control, protect, and isolate high voltage power systems.

Feeder circuit

ID: 602 Level 3

Conductors between the service equipment and the branch circuit overcurrent device.

Energized

ID: 603 Level 1

Electrically connected to a source of voltage or carrying electric current.

Conduit bender

ID: 604 Level 1

A tool used to bend electrical conduit to specific angles for routing.

Junction box

ID: 607 Level 1

An enclosure that protects electrical connections and provides access for maintenance.

Three-phase power

ID: 611 Level 3

A type of electrical power transmission using three conductors with alternating currents offset by 120 degrees.

Emergency lighting

ID: 622 Level 2

Battery-powered lighting that activates automatically when normal power fails to ensure safe evacuation.

Standby generator

ID: 624 Level 2

A backup electrical system that automatically provides power when the main power source fails.

Uninterruptible power supply

ID: 625 Level 3

A device that provides emergency power instantly when the input power source fails using battery backup.

Transfer switch

ID: 626 Level 2

A device that switches electrical load between two sources, typically utility power and backup generator.

Photovoltaic system

ID: 627 Level 2

A system that converts sunlight directly into electricity using solar panels.

Inverter

ID: 628 Level 2

A device that converts direct current (DC) to alternating current (AC).

Rectifier

ID: 629 Level 2

A device that converts alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC).

Transformer

ID: 630 Level 2

A device that transfers electrical energy between circuits through electromagnetic induction, typically changing voltage levels.

Step-down transformer

ID: 631 Level 2

A transformer that reduces voltage from primary to secondary winding.

Step-up transformer

ID: 632 Level 2

A transformer that increases voltage from primary to secondary winding.

Current transformer

ID: 633 Level 3

A device used to measure alternating current by producing a proportional current in its secondary winding.

Potential transformer

ID: 634 Level 3

A device used to measure high voltage by producing a proportional lower voltage in its secondary winding.

Explosion-proof equipment

ID: 636 Level 3

Electrical equipment designed to contain any explosion within its enclosure and prevent ignition of surrounding atmosphere.

Power factor correction

ID: 640 Level 3

The process of adjusting electrical load characteristics to improve power factor using capacitors or other devices.

Lighting control system

ID: 641 Level 2

A system that controls lighting fixtures including dimming, scheduling, and occupancy sensing for energy efficiency.

Dimmer switch

ID: 643 Level 1

A device that controls the brightness of lighting fixtures by varying the voltage or current supplied.

Insulation resistance

ID: 644 Level 2

The resistance offered by insulation material to the flow of electric current, measured in megohms.

Megohmmeter

ID: 645 Level 1

An instrument used to measure insulation resistance by applying a high test voltage to electrical circuits.

Thermal imaging

ID: 646 Level 3

A technique that uses infrared cameras to detect heat patterns and identify electrical hot spots or equipment problems.

Torque specification

ID: 647 Level 3

The recommended tightening force for electrical connections to ensure proper contact and prevent loose connections.

Seismic restraint

ID: 648 Level 2

Equipment and methods used to secure electrical equipment and prevent damage during earthquakes.

Conductor fill

ID: 649 Level 2

The maximum number and size of conductors permitted in a raceway according to electrical code requirements.

Hydro

ID: 650 Level 1

Canadian term for electrical utility company or electrical service (e.g., 'BC Hydro', 'Hydro One'). Also used colloquially to refer to electricity or power service.
