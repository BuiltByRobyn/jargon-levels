Welder Terms Reference

Industry glossary with 50 terms across multiple skill levels

Showing 50 of 50 terms

Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

ID: 460 Level 1

A welding process that uses a consumable electrode covered with flux to lay the weld, commonly known as stick welding.

Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW)

ID: 461 Level 1

A welding process using a continuous solid wire electrode fed into the weld pool, commonly known as MIG welding.

Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW)

ID: 462 Level 2

An arc welding process using a non-consumable tungsten electrode, commonly known as TIG welding.

Flux Cored Arc Welding (FCAW)

ID: 463 Level 1

A semi-automatic arc welding process using a tubular wire filled with flux.

Oxy-Fuel Cutting (OFC)

ID: 464 Level 1

A cutting process using oxygen and fuel gas to preheat and cut metal.

Plasma Arc Cutting (PAC)

ID: 465 Level 1

A cutting process using an accelerated jet of hot plasma to cut electrically conductive materials.

Fillet Weld

ID: 467 Level 1

A weld of triangular cross-section joining two surfaces at right angles in a lap, tee, or corner joint.

Groove Weld

ID: 468 Level 1

A weld made in a groove between two members to be joined, including butt joints with edge preparations.

Root Pass

ID: 469 Level 2

The first weld bead deposited in a multi-pass weld, forming the foundation of the joint.

Cap Pass

ID: 470 Level 2

The final weld pass on the face side of a weld, also called cover pass.

Tack Weld

ID: 471 Level 1

A small weld made to hold parts in proper alignment until final welds are made.

Undercut

ID: 472 Level 1

A groove melted into the base metal adjacent to the weld and left unfilled by weld metal.

Porosity

ID: 473 Level 1

Cavity-type discontinuities formed by gas entrapment during weld metal solidification.

Slag Inclusion

ID: 474 Level 1

Nonmetallic solid material entrapped in weld metal or between weld metal and base metal.

Lack of Fusion

ID: 475 Level 1

A weld discontinuity where fusion did not occur between weld metal and base metal or between weld beads.

Lack of Penetration

ID: 476 Level 1

A joint root condition where weld metal does not extend through the joint thickness.

Heat Affected Zone (HAZ)

ID: 477 Level 2

The portion of base metal whose mechanical properties or microstructure have been altered by welding heat.

Weld Pool

ID: 478 Level 1

The localized volume of molten metal in a weld prior to its solidification.

Electrode

ID: 479 Level 1

A component of the welding circuit through which current is conducted to the arc or base metal.

Filler Metal

ID: 480 Level 1

Metal or alloy added in making a welded, brazed, or soldered joint.

Base Metal

ID: 481 Level 1

The metal or alloy that is welded, brazed, soldered, or cut.

Shielding Gas

ID: 482 Level 1

Protective gas used to prevent or reduce atmospheric contamination of the weld zone.

Arc Blow

ID: 484 Level 2

The deflection of an electric arc from its normal path due to magnetic forces.

Flat Position (1F/1G)

ID: 492 Level 2

The welding position where the weld is performed from the upper side with the face approximately horizontal.

Horizontal Position (2F/2G)

ID: 493 Level 2

The welding position where the weld axis is horizontal and the face is in a vertical plane.

Vertical Position (3F/3G)

ID: 494 Level 2

The welding position where the weld axis is approximately vertical.

Overhead Position (4F/4G)

ID: 495 Level 2

The welding position where welding is performed from the underside of the joint.

Fixed Pipe Position (5G/6G)

ID: 496 Level 3

A pipe welding position where the pipe cannot be rotated and the welder must move around it.

Root Gap

ID: 497 Level 2

The separation at the root between the workpieces to be joined by welding.

Bevel Angle

ID: 499 Level 2

The angle formed between the prepared edge of a member and a plane perpendicular to the surface.

Single-V Groove

ID: 500 Level 3

A weld joint preparation where both pieces are beveled from one side to form a V-shaped groove.

Double-V Groove

ID: 501 Level 3

A weld joint preparation beveled from both sides to form V-shaped grooves on both sides.

J-Groove

ID: 502 Level 3

A weld joint preparation where one member has a groove in the shape of a J.

Backing Strip

ID: 504 Level 3

Material placed at the root of a weld joint to support molten weld metal during welding.

Preheat

ID: 506 Level 2

Heat applied to the base metal immediately before welding to reduce thermal shock and cracking.

Post-Weld Heat Treatment (PWHT)

ID: 508 Level 3

Heat treatment applied after welding to reduce residual stresses and improve mechanical properties.

Guided Bend Test

ID: 514 Level 2

A destructive test where a specimen is bent to a specified radius to evaluate ductility and soundness.

Radiographic Testing (RT)

ID: 518 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using X-rays or gamma rays to detect internal discontinuities in welds.

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

ID: 519 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using high-frequency sound waves to detect internal discontinuities.

Magnetic Particle Testing (MT)

ID: 520 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using magnetic fields and iron particles to detect surface and near-surface discontinuities in ferromagnetic materials.

Dye Penetrant Testing (PT)

ID: 521 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using colored dye to detect surface-breaking discontinuities.

Visual Inspection (VT)

ID: 522 Level 2

The examination of welds using the naked eye or low-power magnification to assess quality and detect surface defects.

Weld Procedure Specification (WPS)

ID: 523 Level 3

A document providing detailed welding parameters and procedures to ensure consistent weld quality.

Procedure Qualification Record (PQR)

ID: 524 Level 3

A document recording the welding data used to weld a test coupon and the test results.

Welder Performance Qualification

ID: 525 Level 3

Testing and documentation to demonstrate a welder's ability to produce sound welds following a specific WPS.

Distortion

ID: 530 Level 2

The dimensional change in a weldment caused by the heating and cooling cycle during welding.

Purge Gas

ID: 533 Level 3

Protective gas used on the backside of a weld to prevent oxidation of the root.

Austenitic Stainless Steel

ID: 534 Level 3

A family of stainless steels with non-magnetic properties containing high chromium and nickel content.

Weldability

ID: 540 Level 2

The capacity of a material to be welded under specified conditions to produce sound joints.

Pipe Schedule

ID: 547 Level 3

A designation system for pipe wall thickness, such as Schedule 40 or Schedule 80.
