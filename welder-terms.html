Welder Terms Reference

Industry glossary with 101 terms across multiple skill levels

Showing 101 of 101 terms

Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

ID: 460 Level 1

A welding process that uses a consumable electrode covered with flux to lay the weld, commonly known as stick welding.

Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW)

ID: 461 Level 1

A welding process using a continuous solid wire electrode fed into the weld pool, commonly known as MIG welding.

Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW)

ID: 462 Level 2

An arc welding process using a non-consumable tungsten electrode, commonly known as TIG welding.

Flux Cored Arc Welding (FCAW)

ID: 463 Level 1

A semi-automatic arc welding process using a tubular wire filled with flux.

Oxy-Fuel Cutting (OFC)

ID: 464 Level 1

A cutting process using oxygen and fuel gas to preheat and cut metal.

Plasma Arc Cutting (PAC)

ID: 465 Level 1

A cutting process using an accelerated jet of hot plasma to cut electrically conductive materials.

Air Carbon Arc Cutting (CAC-A)

ID: 466 Level 1

A cutting and gouging process using compressed air to remove molten metal.

Fillet Weld

ID: 467 Level 1

A weld of triangular cross-section joining two surfaces at right angles in a lap, tee, or corner joint.

Groove Weld

ID: 468 Level 1

A weld made in a groove between two members to be joined, including butt joints with edge preparations.

Root Pass

ID: 469 Level 2

The first weld bead deposited in a multi-pass weld, forming the foundation of the joint.

Cap Pass

ID: 470 Level 2

The final weld pass on the face side of a weld, also called cover pass.

Tack Weld

ID: 471 Level 1

A small weld made to hold parts in proper alignment until final welds are made.

Undercut

ID: 472 Level 1

A groove melted into the base metal adjacent to the weld and left unfilled by weld metal.

Porosity

ID: 473 Level 1

Cavity-type discontinuities formed by gas entrapment during weld metal solidification.

Slag Inclusion

ID: 474 Level 1

Nonmetallic solid material entrapped in weld metal or between weld metal and base metal.

Lack of Fusion

ID: 475 Level 1

A weld discontinuity where fusion did not occur between weld metal and base metal or between weld beads.

Lack of Penetration

ID: 476 Level 1

A joint root condition where weld metal does not extend through the joint thickness.

Heat Affected Zone (HAZ)

ID: 477 Level 2

The portion of base metal whose mechanical properties or microstructure have been altered by welding heat.

Weld Pool

ID: 478 Level 1

The localized volume of molten metal in a weld prior to its solidification.

Electrode

ID: 479 Level 1

A component of the welding circuit through which current is conducted to the arc or base metal.

Filler Metal

ID: 480 Level 1

Metal or alloy added in making a welded, brazed, or soldered joint.

Base Metal

ID: 481 Level 1

The metal or alloy that is welded, brazed, soldered, or cut.

Shielding Gas

ID: 482 Level 1

Protective gas used to prevent or reduce atmospheric contamination of the weld zone.

Polarity

ID: 483 Level 1

The direction of current flow in a DC welding circuit, either electrode positive or electrode negative.

Arc Blow

ID: 484 Level 2

The deflection of an electric arc from its normal path due to magnetic forces.

Duty Cycle

ID: 485 Level 1

The percentage of time a welding power source can operate at rated output without overheating.

Wire Feed Speed

ID: 486 Level 1

The rate at which electrode wire is fed through the welding gun in semi-automatic processes.

Travel Speed

ID: 487 Level 1

The linear rate at which the welding arc is moved along the weld joint.

Amperage

ID: 488 Level 1

The measurement of electrical current flow in the welding circuit, controlling heat input and penetration.

Voltage

ID: 489 Level 1

The electrical potential difference in a welding circuit, affecting arc length and weld bead profile.

Stringer Bead

ID: 490 Level 1

A weld bead made without weaving motion, deposited in a straight line.

Weave Bead

ID: 491 Level 1

A weld bead made with a side-to-side oscillating motion to produce a wider bead.

Flat Position (1F/1G)

ID: 492 Level 1

The welding position where the weld is performed from the upper side with the face approximately horizontal.

Horizontal Position (2F/2G)

ID: 493 Level 1

The welding position where the weld axis is horizontal and the face is in a vertical plane.

Vertical Position (3F/3G)

ID: 494 Level 1

The welding position where the weld axis is approximately vertical.

Overhead Position (4F/4G)

ID: 495 Level 2

The welding position where welding is performed from the underside of the joint.

Fixed Pipe Position (5G/6G)

ID: 496 Level 2

A pipe welding position where the pipe cannot be rotated and the welder must move around it.

Root Gap

ID: 497 Level 2

The separation at the root between the workpieces to be joined by welding.

Root Opening

ID: 498 Level 2

The distance between members to be joined at the root of the joint.

Bevel Angle

ID: 499 Level 2

The angle formed between the prepared edge of a member and a plane perpendicular to the surface.

Single-V Groove

ID: 500 Level 2

A weld joint preparation where both pieces are beveled from one side to form a V-shaped groove.

Double-V Groove

ID: 501 Level 2

A weld joint preparation beveled from both sides to form V-shaped grooves on both sides.

J-Groove

ID: 502 Level 2

A weld joint preparation where one member has a groove in the shape of a J.

U-Groove

ID: 503 Level 2

A weld joint preparation where both members have edges shaped to form a U-shaped groove.

Backing Strip

ID: 504 Level 2

Material placed at the root of a weld joint to support molten weld metal during welding.

Back Gouging

ID: 505 Level 2

The removal of weld metal and base metal from the other side of a joint to facilitate complete fusion.

Preheat

ID: 506 Level 2

Heat applied to the base metal immediately before welding to reduce thermal shock and cracking.

Interpass Temperature

ID: 507 Level 2

The temperature range of the base metal between weld passes in a multi-pass weld.

Post-Weld Heat Treatment (PWHT)

ID: 508 Level 3

Heat treatment applied after welding to reduce residual stresses and improve mechanical properties.

Weld Reinforcement

ID: 509 Level 2

Weld metal in excess of the quantity required to fill a joint, extending beyond the base metal surface.

Throat Thickness

ID: 510 Level 2

The shortest distance from the root of a fillet weld to its face, indicating effective weld size.

Leg Length

ID: 511 Level 2

The distance from the root of a fillet weld to the toe on each welded member.

Weld Toe

ID: 512 Level 2

The junction between the face of a weld and the base metal.

Weld Root

ID: 513 Level 2

The points at which the back of the weld intersects the base metal surfaces.

Guided Bend Test

ID: 514 Level 2

A destructive test where a specimen is bent to a specified radius to evaluate ductility and soundness.

Face Bend Test

ID: 515 Level 2

A guided bend test with the weld face in tension, used to detect surface defects.

Root Bend Test

ID: 516 Level 2

A guided bend test with the weld root in tension, used to detect root defects.

Side Bend Test

ID: 517 Level 2

A guided bend test where the specimen is bent with the weld perpendicular to the direction of bending.

Radiographic Testing (RT)

ID: 518 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using X-rays or gamma rays to detect internal discontinuities in welds.

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

ID: 519 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using high-frequency sound waves to detect internal discontinuities.

Magnetic Particle Testing (MT)

ID: 520 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using magnetic fields and iron particles to detect surface and near-surface discontinuities in ferromagnetic materials.

Dye Penetrant Testing (PT)

ID: 521 Level 3

A non-destructive testing method using colored dye to detect surface-breaking discontinuities.

Visual Inspection (VT)

ID: 522 Level 2

The examination of welds using the naked eye or low-power magnification to assess quality and detect surface defects.

Weld Procedure Specification (WPS)

ID: 523 Level 3

A document providing detailed welding parameters and procedures to ensure consistent weld quality.

Procedure Qualification Record (PQR)

ID: 524 Level 3

A document recording the welding data used to weld a test coupon and the test results.

Welder Performance Qualification

ID: 525 Level 3

Testing and documentation to demonstrate a welder's ability to produce sound welds following a specific WPS.

Heat Affected Zone (HAZ)

ID: 526 Level 2

The area of base metal that has had its microstructure and properties altered by the heat of welding.

Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

ID: 527 Level 2

A manual arc welding process using a consumable electrode coated with flux to protect the weld area.

Submerged Arc Welding (SAW)

ID: 528 Level 2

An automatic welding process where the arc is submerged beneath a blanket of granular flux.

Metal Cored Arc Welding (MCAW)

ID: 529 Level 2

A semi-automatic welding process using tubular wire filled with metal powders for high deposition rates.

Distortion

ID: 530 Level 2

The dimensional change in a weldment caused by the heating and cooling cycle during welding.

Spatter

ID: 531 Level 1

Metal particles expelled during welding that do not form part of the weld.

Consumable Insert

ID: 532 Level 3

Pre-placed filler metal at the weld root that becomes part of the weld during welding.

Purge Gas

ID: 533 Level 3

Protective gas used on the backside of a weld to prevent oxidation of the root.

Austenitic Stainless Steel

ID: 534 Level 3

A family of stainless steels with non-magnetic properties containing high chromium and nickel content.

Low Hydrogen Electrode

ID: 535 Level 2

A welding electrode with a coating that produces minimal hydrogen in the weld, reducing the risk of hydrogen cracking.

Hardsurfacing

ID: 536 Level 1

The application of a hard, wear-resistant material to a surface by welding to extend component life.

Gouging

ID: 537 Level 1

The process of removing metal by creating a groove or channel in the base metal.

Torch Angle

ID: 538 Level 1

The angle at which the welding torch is held relative to the workpiece during welding.

Arc Length

ID: 539 Level 1

The distance from the tip of the electrode to the surface of the weld pool.

Weldability

ID: 540 Level 2

The capacity of a material to be welded under specified conditions to produce sound joints.

Aluminum Alloy

ID: 541 Level 3

A metal composed primarily of aluminum with added elements to enhance properties for welding applications.

Oxyacetylene Welding

ID: 542 Level 1

A welding process using oxygen and acetylene gases to produce a flame for melting and joining metals.

Braze Welding

ID: 543 Level 1

A joining process using a brass or bronze filler metal with a melting point above 450¬∞C but below the base metal.

Flux

ID: 544 Level 1

A material used to prevent, dissolve, or facilitate removal of oxides and other undesirable surface substances.

Downhill Welding

ID: 545 Level 1

A welding technique where progression is from top to bottom in the vertical position.

Uphill Welding

ID: 546 Level 2

A welding technique where progression is from bottom to top in the vertical position.

Pipe Schedule

ID: 547 Level 3

A designation system for pipe wall thickness, such as Schedule 40 or Schedule 80.

Butt Joint

ID: 548 Level 1

A joint between two members aligned approximately in the same plane.

Lap Joint

ID: 549 Level 1

A joint between two overlapping members in parallel planes.

Tee Joint

ID: 550 Level 1

A joint between two members intersecting at approximately right angles.

Corner Joint

ID: 551 Level 1

A joint between two members located approximately at right angles to each other forming an L-shape.

Edge Joint

ID: 552 Level 1

A joint between the edges of two or more parallel or nearly parallel members.

Weld Bead

ID: 553 Level 1

A weld resulting from a single pass of the welding electrode or torch.

Multi-Pass Weld

ID: 554 Level 2

A weld made by depositing filler metal in two or more passes or layers.

Work Hardening

ID: 555 Level 3

An increase in hardness and strength caused by plastic deformation at temperatures below the recrystallization range.

Normalizing

ID: 556 Level 3

A heat treatment process for steel where the material is heated and then air-cooled to refine grain structure.

Annealing

ID: 557 Level 3

A heat treatment process that alters the microstructure to soften the material and improve ductility.

Tempering

ID: 558 Level 3

A heat treatment process performed after hardening to reduce brittleness and improve toughness.

Welding Symbol

ID: 559 Level 1

A graphical representation on drawings that conveys complete welding information using standardized symbols.

Welder Logbook

ID: 560 Level 1

A document recording work-based training hours, tasks completed, and skills developed during welding apprenticeship.
