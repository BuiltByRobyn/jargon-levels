Mechanic Terms Reference

Industry glossary with 100 terms across multiple skill levels

Showing 100 of 100 terms

Cant

ID: 47 Level 2

A slight slope or tilt built into a surface.

Catastrophic Failure

ID: 49 Level 2

A sudden and complete breakdown of a system or structure.

Keyway

ID: 155 Level 2

A slot or groove designed to fit a key for locking parts together.

Rubblization

ID: 241 Level 3

The process of breaking old concrete pavement into small pieces for reuse.

Aviation Snips

ID: 307 Level 1

Cutting tools used to cut sheet metal.

Pry Bar

ID: 308 Level 1

A tool used to force objects apart or remove nails.

Wrecking Bar

ID: 309 Level 2

A heavy bar used for demolition and pulling apart materials.

Aligning Bar

ID: 310 Level 2

A bar used to line up holes or move heavy objects into place.

Cone/Tie Wrench

ID: 322 Level 2

A wrench used to tighten or loosen nuts on tie rods or cones.

Slide Hammer

ID: 382 Level 2

A tool with a sliding weight used to pull out parts like bearings.

Spud Wrench

ID: 385 Level 2

A wrench with a tapered spike on one end, used for aligning holes.

Torque wrench

ID: 651 Level 1

A wrench used to apply a specific amount of torque to a fastener, ensuring proper tightening according to manufacturer specifications.

Feeler gauge

ID: 652 Level 1

A precision measurement tool consisting of thin metal blades of varying thicknesses used to measure gap widths between parts.

Impact wrench

ID: 653 Level 1

A power tool that delivers high torque output with minimal exertion by the user, using rotational impacts to loosen or tighten fasteners.

Micrometer

ID: 654 Level 1

A precision measuring instrument used to measure small distances or thicknesses with high accuracy, typically to within 0.001 inch or 0.01 millimeter.

Dial indicator

ID: 655 Level 2

A precision measuring tool that uses a dial and needle to indicate small linear distances or variations in dimensions, commonly used to check runout and endplay.

Compression tester

ID: 656 Level 2

A diagnostic tool used to measure the compression pressure in an engine's cylinders to assess engine condition and identify mechanical problems.

Cylinder leak down tester

ID: 657 Level 2

A diagnostic tool that measures the percentage of air pressure loss in a cylinder to identify internal engine leaks through valves, piston rings, or head gasket.

Scan tool

ID: 658 Level 1

An electronic diagnostic device that interfaces with a vehicle's on-board computer systems to retrieve trouble codes, sensor data, and perform system tests.

Digital Volt-Ohm Meter (DVOM)

ID: 659 Level 1

A digital multimeter used to measure voltage, current (amperage), and resistance (ohms) in electrical circuits and components.

Oscilloscope (Lab scope)

ID: 660 Level 3

An electronic test instrument that displays voltage signals as waveforms over time, used to diagnose electrical and electronic system problems.

Catalytic converter

ID: 661 Level 2

An exhaust emission control device that converts toxic pollutants in exhaust gas into less harmful substances through chemical reactions.

Throttle body

ID: 662 Level 2

A component in the air intake system that controls the amount of air flowing into the engine based on accelerator pedal input.

Crankshaft

ID: 663 Level 2

A rotating shaft that converts the reciprocating motion of the pistons into rotational motion to power the vehicle.

Camshaft

ID: 664 Level 2

A rotating shaft with lobes that controls the opening and closing of engine valves to regulate air and fuel intake and exhaust flow.

Radiator

ID: 665 Level 2

A heat exchanger that cools engine coolant by transferring heat to the surrounding air through metal fins and tubes.

Timing belt

ID: 666 Level 2

A toothed belt that synchronizes the rotation of the crankshaft and camshaft to ensure proper engine valve timing.

Fuel injector

ID: 667 Level 2

An electronically controlled valve that precisely sprays fuel into the engine's combustion chamber or intake manifold.

Oxygen sensor

ID: 668 Level 2

A sensor that measures the amount of oxygen in the exhaust gases to help the engine control module adjust the air-fuel mixture for optimal combustion and emissions.

Mass air flow sensor

ID: 669 Level 2

A sensor that measures the mass of air entering the engine to help the engine control module calculate the proper amount of fuel needed for combustion.

Serpentine belt

ID: 670 Level 2

A continuous ribbed belt that drives multiple engine accessories such as the alternator, power steering pump, air conditioning compressor, and water pump from a single pulley on the crankshaft.

Alternator

ID: 671 Level 2

An electrical generator that converts mechanical energy from the engine into electrical energy to charge the battery and power the vehicle's electrical systems while the engine is running.

Starter motor

ID: 672 Level 2

An electric motor that cranks the engine to initiate the combustion process when starting the vehicle.

Turbocharger

ID: 673 Level 2

A forced induction device driven by exhaust gases that compresses intake air to increase engine power output and efficiency.

Piston

ID: 674 Level 2

A cylindrical component that moves up and down within the engine cylinder, compressing the air-fuel mixture and transferring combustion force to the crankshaft through the connecting rod.

Connecting rod

ID: 675 Level 2

A metal rod that connects the piston to the crankshaft, converting the piston's linear motion into rotational motion.

Thermostat

ID: 676 Level 2

A temperature-controlled valve in the cooling system that regulates coolant flow to maintain optimal engine operating temperature.

Water pump

ID: 677 Level 2

A mechanical pump that circulates coolant through the engine and cooling system to prevent overheating.

Intake manifold

ID: 678 Level 2

A system of passages that distributes the air or air-fuel mixture from the throttle body to the individual cylinders of the engine.

Exhaust manifold

ID: 679 Level 2

A component that collects exhaust gases from multiple cylinders and channels them into the exhaust system.

Transmission fluid

ID: 680 Level 2

A specialized lubricant that provides lubrication, cooling, and hydraulic pressure for transmission operation and protects internal components from wear.

Brake caliper

ID: 681 Level 2

A hydraulic clamp-like device that holds the brake pads and squeezes them against the brake rotor to create friction and slow the vehicle.

Brake rotor

ID: 682 Level 2

A disc-shaped metal component attached to the wheel that the brake pads press against to create friction and slow the vehicle.

Brake pad

ID: 683 Level 2

A friction material mounted in the brake caliper that presses against the brake rotor to generate stopping force through friction.

CV joint

ID: 684 Level 2

A constant velocity joint that allows a drive shaft to transmit power through a variable angle at constant rotational speed without friction increase.

Differential

ID: 685 Level 2

A gear system that allows the drive wheels to rotate at different speeds while cornering, enabling smooth turns and reducing tire wear.

Universal joint

ID: 686 Level 2

A flexible coupling that allows a drive shaft to transmit rotational motion between two shafts that are not in a straight line.

Power steering pump

ID: 687 Level 2

A hydraulic pump that provides pressurized fluid to assist the steering system, reducing the effort needed to turn the steering wheel.

Strut assembly

ID: 688 Level 2

A suspension component that combines a shock absorber and coil spring into a single structural unit that supports the vehicle's weight and dampens road impacts.

Ball joint

ID: 689 Level 2

A flexible pivot point in the suspension system that connects the control arm to the steering knuckle, allowing for smooth steering and suspension movement.

Hydraulic modulator

ID: 690 Level 2

A component in anti-lock braking systems that controls brake fluid pressure to individual wheels to prevent wheel lockup during braking.

Valve train assembly

ID: 691 Level 2

The mechanical system that operates the intake and exhaust valves, including components such as camshaft, lifters, pushrods, rocker arms, and valve springs.

Torque converter

ID: 692 Level 2

A fluid coupling device in automatic transmissions that transfers rotating power from the engine to the transmission while allowing the engine to continue running when the vehicle is stopped.

Planetary gear set

ID: 693 Level 3

A gear system consisting of a central sun gear, multiple planet gears, and a ring gear that provides multiple gear ratios in automatic transmissions through different holding combinations.

Exhaust gas recirculation (EGR)

ID: 694 Level 3

An emissions control system that reduces nitrogen oxide emissions by recirculating a portion of exhaust gases back into the engine's combustion chambers to lower combustion temperatures.

Controller Area Network (CAN bus)

ID: 695 Level 3

A vehicle communication protocol that allows electronic control modules throughout a vehicle to communicate with each other without a central computer, using twisted pair wiring.

Pulse width modulation (PWM)

ID: 696 Level 3

A method of controlling electrical power to devices by rapidly switching the power on and off, with the ratio of on-time to off-time determining the effective power delivered.

Synchronizer

ID: 697 Level 2

A friction device in manual transmissions that matches the rotational speed of gears before engagement, allowing smooth shifting without gear grinding.

Diesel particulate filter (DPF)

ID: 698 Level 2

An emissions control device that captures and stores soot and particulate matter from diesel exhaust, periodically burning it off through a regeneration process.

Selective catalytic reduction (SCR)

ID: 699 Level 3

An emissions control technology that injects diesel exhaust fluid into exhaust gases to convert nitrogen oxides into harmless nitrogen and water vapor through a catalytic reaction.

Common rail injection

ID: 700 Level 3

A diesel fuel injection system that uses a high-pressure accumulator rail to supply fuel to electronically controlled injectors, allowing precise control of injection timing and quantity.

Variable valve timing (VVT)

ID: 701 Level 2

An engine technology that adjusts the timing of valve opening and closing to optimize engine performance, fuel efficiency, and emissions across different operating conditions.

Evaporative emission control system (EVAP)

ID: 702 Level 3

A system that captures and stores fuel vapors from the fuel tank and prevents them from escaping into the atmosphere, routing them instead to the engine for combustion.

Knock sensor

ID: 703 Level 3

A sensor that detects abnormal combustion (knocking or pinging) in the engine and sends signals to the engine control module to adjust ignition timing and prevent engine damage.

Flywheel

ID: 704 Level 2

A heavy rotating disc attached to the crankshaft that stores rotational energy, smooths out engine power pulses, and provides a friction surface for the clutch in manual transmissions.

Throttle position sensor (TPS)

ID: 705 Level 3

A sensor that monitors the position of the throttle valve and sends this information to the engine control module to determine fuel delivery and ignition timing based on driver demand.

Wheel speed sensor

ID: 706 Level 2

A sensor that monitors the rotational speed of each wheel and provides data to anti-lock braking, traction control, and stability control systems to optimize vehicle safety and performance.

Proportioning valve

ID: 707 Level 2

A brake system component that regulates hydraulic pressure between front and rear brakes to prevent rear wheel lockup during braking and maintain proper brake balance.

Tie rod end

ID: 708 Level 1

A ball-and-socket joint at the end of a tie rod that connects the steering system to the steering knuckle, allowing for steering movement while accommodating suspension travel.

Master cylinder

ID: 709 Level 1

The primary hydraulic component in a brake system that converts mechanical force from the brake pedal into hydraulic pressure to actuate the brakes at all wheels.

MacPherson strut

ID: 710 Level 1

A common independent front suspension design that combines a shock absorber and coil spring into a single structural unit, serving as both a suspension component and steering pivot.

Control arm

ID: 711 Level 1

A suspension component that connects the wheel hub to the vehicle frame, allowing vertical wheel movement while maintaining proper wheel alignment and controlling suspension geometry.

Sway bar (Anti-roll bar)

ID: 712 Level 1

A torsion spring that connects opposite wheels together through suspension components to reduce body roll during cornering and improve handling stability.

Bushing

ID: 713 Level 1

A rubber or polyurethane cushion that isolates and dampens vibrations while allowing controlled movement between suspension and chassis components.

Idler arm

ID: 714 Level 1

A pivoting support in parallelogram steering linkage that mirrors the pitman arm movement and maintains proper geometry in the steering system.

Pitman arm

ID: 715 Level 1

A lever arm connected to the steering box sector shaft that converts rotational motion from the steering wheel into linear motion to move the steering linkage.

Rack and pinion

ID: 716 Level 1

A steering system design that uses a circular pinion gear meshing with a linear rack to directly convert steering wheel rotation into lateral movement of the tie rods.

Caster

ID: 717 Level 1

The forward or rearward tilt of the steering axis when viewed from the side, affecting steering stability and wheel return-to-center characteristics.

Camber

ID: 718 Level 1

The inward or outward tilt of the wheel from vertical when viewed from the front, affecting tire wear and cornering performance.

Toe

ID: 719 Level 1

The angle at which the front of the wheels point inward (toe-in) or outward (toe-out) when viewed from above, affecting tire wear and straight-line stability.

Driveshaft (Propeller shaft)

ID: 720 Level 1

A rotating shaft that transmits torque from the transmission to the differential in rear-wheel and four-wheel drive vehicles, typically incorporating universal joints to accommodate suspension movement.

Viscous coupling

ID: 721 Level 3

A torque transfer device that uses silicone fluid between closely spaced plates to automatically distribute power between axles in all-wheel drive systems based on speed differences.

Limited slip differential

ID: 722 Level 2

A differential that limits the speed difference between two wheels on the same axle, providing better traction by transferring torque to the wheel with more grip when one wheel starts to slip.

Ring gear

ID: 723 Level 2

A large circular gear in the differential that meshes with the pinion gear to transmit power at a 90-degree angle and multiply torque to the drive wheels.

Pinion gear

ID: 724 Level 2

A small gear attached to the driveshaft that meshes with the ring gear in the differential to change the direction of power flow and provide gear reduction.

Axle shaft

ID: 725 Level 1

A rotating shaft that transmits torque from the differential to the drive wheels, connecting the differential side gears to the wheel hubs.

Bearing preload

ID: 726 Level 2

A controlled amount of tension applied to bearings during assembly to eliminate internal clearance, reduce wear, and improve rotational accuracy and stiffness.

Backlash

ID: 727 Level 2

The amount of clearance or play between meshing gear teeth, which must be properly adjusted to prevent noise and ensure smooth operation while allowing for thermal expansion.

Bearing cap

ID: 728 Level 2

A removable cover that secures bearings in place within a housing, providing access for installation, adjustment, and maintenance while maintaining proper bearing alignment.

Shim

ID: 729 Level 2

A thin piece of material used to fill small gaps, adjust clearances, or set precise measurements during assembly and adjustment of mechanical components.

Spline

ID: 730 Level 2

A series of ridges or teeth on a shaft that mesh with corresponding grooves in a hub, allowing torque transmission while permitting axial movement between components.

Runout

ID: 731 Level 1

The amount of wobble or deviation from true circular rotation in a rotating component, measured to ensure proper balance and alignment.

Endplay

ID: 732 Level 2

The amount of axial or end-to-end movement allowed in a rotating shaft or component, which must be within specifications to prevent noise and premature wear.

Gear ratio

ID: 733 Level 2

The relationship between the number of teeth on two meshing gears or the ratio of input speed to output speed, determining torque multiplication and speed reduction.

Overdrive

ID: 734 Level 2

A gear ratio where the output shaft rotates faster than the input shaft, reducing engine RPM at highway speeds to improve fuel economy and reduce engine wear.

Transaxle

ID: 735 Level 2

An integrated assembly combining the transmission, differential, and drive axles into a single unit, commonly used in front-wheel drive and some rear-engine vehicles.

Viscosity

ID: 736 Level 2

The measure of a fluid's resistance to flow and its thickness, critical for proper lubrication as it affects how well oil protects engine components under various temperature conditions.

Volatility

ID: 737 Level 3

The tendency of a fuel or fluid to vaporize, affecting cold-start performance, emissions, and evaporative losses in fuel systems.

Cetane rating

ID: 738 Level 2

A measurement of diesel fuel's ignition quality and combustion speed, with higher numbers indicating easier ignition and smoother engine operation.

Octane rating

ID: 739 Level 3

A measure of gasoline's resistance to premature ignition or knocking, with higher numbers indicating better resistance to engine knock and allowing higher compression ratios.
