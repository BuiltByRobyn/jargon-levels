Backer Rod

ID: 19 Level 2

A flexible foam rod placed in joints before sealing to control sealant depth.

Caulking

ID: 50 Level 1

Material used to seal joints or seams against leaks.

Embankment

ID: 95 Level 2

A raised structure of earth or stone built to hold back water or support a road.

Moling

ID: 183 Level 2

A method of laying pipes underground without digging trenches.

PVC

ID: 225 Level 1

A plastic material commonly used for pipes and fittings.

Volume

ID: 291 Level 2

The amount of space an object or substance occupies.

Waterproofing

ID: 294 Level 3

The process of making a surface resistant to water.

Caulking Gun

ID: 316 Level 1

A tool that holds tubes of caulk and applies it to seal gaps.

Pipe Wrench

ID: 363 Level 1

A wrench with serrated jaws used for turning pipes.

Backflow Preventer

ID: 740 Level 2

A device that prevents contaminated water from flowing back into the potable water supply.

Cleanout

ID: 741 Level 1

A fitting with a removable plug that provides access to drain lines for cleaning blockages.

Trap

ID: 742 Level 1

A curved section of pipe that holds water to prevent sewer gases from entering the building.

Vent Stack

ID: 743 Level 2

A vertical pipe that allows air to enter the drainage system and prevents trap siphonage.

Fixture Unit

ID: 744 Level 2

A measure of the probable discharge into the drainage system by various plumbing fixtures.

Ball Valve

ID: 745 Level 1

A valve with a spherical disc that controls flow through a quarter-turn rotation.

Gate Valve

ID: 746 Level 1

A valve that uses a sliding gate to control flow, typically used for full open or full closed positions.

Check Valve

ID: 747 Level 1

A valve that allows flow in only one direction and prevents backflow.

Pressure Relief Valve

ID: 748 Level 2

A safety valve that automatically releases pressure when it exceeds a set limit.

Pressure Reducing Valve

ID: 749 Level 2

A valve that automatically reduces incoming water pressure to a lower, safer level.

Septic Tank

ID: 750 Level 3

An underground tank that treats wastewater through bacterial decomposition and settling.

Drain Field

ID: 751 Level 3

An area of perforated pipes buried in gravel trenches that disperses wastewater into the soil.

Sump Pump

ID: 752 Level 2

A pump installed in a sump pit that removes accumulated water from basements or crawl spaces.

Water Hammer

ID: 753 Level 2

A pressure surge that creates a loud banging noise when water flow is suddenly stopped.

Gasket

ID: 755 Level 1

A shaped piece of rubber or fiber used to seal joints between pipes or fittings.

Fixture Drain

ID: 756 Level 2

The pipe that carries wastewater from a fixture to the branch drain or soil stack.

Soil Stack

ID: 757 Level 2

A vertical pipe that carries waste from toilets and other fixtures to the building drain.

Branch Drain

ID: 758 Level 2

A horizontal drainage pipe that collects waste from fixture drains and connects to the main drain.

Building Drain

ID: 759 Level 2

The lowest horizontal drain pipe that receives waste from all drainage pipes inside the building.

Building Sewer

ID: 760 Level 2

The pipe that extends from the building drain to the public sewer or private disposal system.

Expansion Tank

ID: 761 Level 2

A tank that absorbs excess pressure caused by thermal expansion in a closed water heating system.

Circulating Pump

ID: 762 Level 2

A pump that moves water through a hydronic heating or cooling system.

Manifold

ID: 763 Level 2

A fitting with multiple outlets that distributes water or gas to several branch lines.

Tempering Valve

ID: 764 Level 2

A valve that mixes hot and cold water to maintain a safe, constant outlet temperature.

Dielectric Union

ID: 765 Level 2

A fitting that prevents galvanic corrosion between dissimilar metals in a piping system.

Hanger

ID: 766 Level 1

A device used to support and secure pipes to the structure of a building.

Sanitary Tee

ID: 767 Level 1

A drainage fitting with a curved branch inlet that allows smooth waste flow in drainage systems.

Wye Fitting

ID: 768 Level 1

A drainage fitting shaped like the letter Y that joins two pipes at a 45-degree angle.

Air Gap

ID: 769 Level 2

An unobstructed vertical space between a water outlet and the flood level rim of a fixture.

Cleanout Plug

ID: 770 Level 1

A removable plug that provides access to a drain line for cleaning or clearing blockages.

Condensate Drain

ID: 771 Level 2

A pipe that removes water produced by condensation from heating or cooling equipment.

Rough-in

ID: 772 Level 1

The installation of pipes and fittings before walls and floors are finished.

Tailpiece

ID: 773 Level 1

A short section of pipe connecting a fixture outlet to the trap.

Slip Joint

ID: 774 Level 1

A mechanical joint that allows for adjustment and easy disconnection using a compression nut and washer.

Stop Valve

ID: 775 Level 1

A valve used to shut off water supply to a fixture or section of pipe.

Drain Snake

ID: 776 Level 1

A flexible tool used to clear blockages in drain lines by rotating through the pipe.

Plunger

ID: 777 Level 1

A rubber cup on a handle used to clear blockages by creating suction and pressure.

Aerator

ID: 778 Level 1

A device attached to a faucet outlet that mixes air with water to reduce splashing and conserve water.

Ferrule

ID: 779 Level 2

A compression ring that seals a pipe connection when tightened.

Bushing

ID: 780 Level 1

A fitting used to connect pipes of different sizes or to reduce the size of a pipe opening.

P-Trap

ID: 781 Level 1

A U-shaped pipe trap that holds standing water to prevent sewer gases from entering buildings.

Soldering

ID: 782 Level 1

The process of joining metal pipes using molten solder and heat below 840°F.

Brazing

ID: 783 Level 2

A metal-joining process using filler metal with a melting point above 840°F but below the melting point of the base metals.

Flux

ID: 784 Level 1

A chemical cleaning agent applied to metal surfaces before soldering or brazing to remove oxidation and improve bonding.

Threading Die

ID: 785 Level 1

A tool used to cut external threads on pipes to create threaded connections.

Pipe Reamer

ID: 786 Level 1

A tool used to remove burrs and smooth the inside edge of a cut pipe.

Tube Bender

ID: 787 Level 1

A tool used to bend tubing to precise angles without kinking or collapsing the pipe.

Nipple

ID: 788 Level 1

A short length of pipe threaded on both ends used to connect two fittings.

Union

ID: 789 Level 1

A threaded fitting that allows pipes to be easily disconnected without cutting or rotating the pipes.

Coupling

ID: 790 Level 1

A short fitting used to connect two pipes of the same diameter in a straight line.

Elbow

ID: 791 Level 1

A pipe fitting with a bend, typically 45 or 90 degrees, used to change the direction of pipe runs.

Reducer

ID: 792 Level 1

A fitting that connects pipes of different diameters, allowing transition from larger to smaller pipe size.

Cap

ID: 793 Level 1

A fitting used to close off the end of a pipe, creating a dead end.

Plug

ID: 794 Level 1

A threaded fitting used to close off an opening in a pipe or fitting, typically removable for access.

Cross

ID: 795 Level 2

A four-way pipe fitting with outlets at 90-degree angles, forming a cross shape.

Street Elbow

ID: 796 Level 2

An elbow fitting with male threads on one end and female threads on the other, used for tight-space installations.

Compression Fitting

ID: 797 Level 2

A mechanical fitting that creates a watertight seal by compressing a ferrule onto the pipe using a compression nut.

Flare Fitting

ID: 798 Level 2

A mechanical connection using a cone-shaped flared end on tubing that seals against a matching fitting.

Sweat Fitting

ID: 799 Level 2

A copper fitting designed to be joined by soldering, where solder is heated and drawn into the joint by capillary action.

ABS Pipe

ID: 800 Level 1

Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene pipe, a black plastic pipe commonly used for drain, waste, and vent systems.

CPVC Pipe

ID: 801 Level 2

Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride pipe, a beige plastic pipe that can handle higher temperatures than PVC, used for hot and cold water distribution.

PEX Pipe

ID: 802 Level 2

Cross-linked Polyethylene pipe, a flexible plastic tubing used for water supply lines that can expand and contract without cracking.

Cast Iron Pipe

ID: 803 Level 2

A durable metal pipe traditionally used for drain, waste, and vent systems, known for its sound-dampening properties.

Galvanized Pipe

ID: 804 Level 2

Steel pipe coated with zinc to resist corrosion, formerly used for water supply but now largely replaced by other materials.

Black Iron Pipe

ID: 805 Level 2

Uncoated steel pipe commonly used for natural gas and propane distribution systems.

Copper Tubing

ID: 806 Level 1

Metal tubing available in rigid or flexible forms, commonly used for water supply and refrigeration lines.

Pipe Dope

ID: 807 Level 1

A sealing compound applied to threaded pipe joints to prevent leaks and lubricate threads during assembly.

Teflon Tape

ID: 808 Level 1

A thin white tape wrapped around pipe threads to create a watertight seal on threaded connections.

Escutcheon

ID: 809 Level 1

A decorative plate that covers the hole where a pipe passes through a wall, floor, or ceiling.

Shut-off Valve

ID: 810 Level 1

A valve installed on a water supply line that allows complete stoppage of water flow to a fixture or system.

Globe Valve

ID: 811 Level 2

A valve that regulates flow in a pipeline by moving a disc perpendicular to the flow, allowing precise flow control.

Butterfly Valve

ID: 812 Level 2

A quarter-turn valve with a rotating disc that controls flow, commonly used in large diameter pipes.

Vacuum Breaker

ID: 813 Level 2

A backflow prevention device that allows air into a water line to prevent backsiphonage when negative pressure occurs.

Water Closet

ID: 814 Level 1

A plumbing fixture consisting of a toilet bowl with a flushing mechanism, also called a toilet.

Flushometer

ID: 815 Level 2

A valve that provides a fixed volume of water for flushing toilets and urinals, commonly used in commercial applications.

Ballcock

ID: 816 Level 2

A float-operated valve that controls water flow into a toilet tank, shutting off when the proper level is reached.

Fill Valve

ID: 817 Level 2

A modern replacement for the ballcock that refills a toilet tank after flushing, using a float cup mechanism.

Flapper

ID: 818 Level 1

A rubber valve at the bottom of a toilet tank that lifts to allow water to flow into the bowl during flushing.

Overflow Tube

ID: 819 Level 2

A vertical pipe in a toilet tank that prevents overflow by directing excess water into the bowl if the fill valve fails.

Wax Ring

ID: 820 Level 1

A wax seal placed between the toilet base and the floor flange to create a watertight connection to the drain.

Closet Flange

ID: 821 Level 2

A pipe fitting that connects a toilet to the drain pipe and provides a mounting surface for the toilet base.

Drum Trap

ID: 822 Level 3

An outdated cylindrical trap with a removable cover, formerly used under bathtubs, now prohibited in most codes.

Fixture Unit

ID: 823 Level 2

A standardized measure used to calculate the total discharge load on a drainage system from multiple fixtures.

Sanitary Tee

ID: 824 Level 1

A drainage fitting with a curved branch that allows smooth waste flow, required in drainage systems where waste changes direction.

Vent Pipe

ID: 825 Level 1

A pipe that allows air to enter the drainage system to prevent trap siphonage and maintain proper drainage flow.

Wet Vent

ID: 826 Level 3

A pipe that serves as both a vent and a drain, allowing fixtures to share common venting under specific conditions.

Loop Vent

ID: 827 Level 3

A vent arrangement that serves multiple fixtures connected to a horizontal branch drain near the stack.

Common Vent

ID: 828 Level 3

A vent connecting at the junction of two fixture drains and serving as a vent for both fixtures.

Individual Vent

ID: 829 Level 2

A pipe that vents a single trapped fixture and connects to the vent system or extends through the roof.

Stack Vent

ID: 830 Level 2

The extension of a soil or waste stack above the highest horizontal drain connected to it, providing ventilation.

Grease Trap

ID: 831 Level 2

A plumbing device that intercepts fats, oils, and grease from wastewater before entering the drainage system.

Air Admittance Valve

ID: 832 Level 3

A one-way valve that allows air to enter the drainage system but prevents sewer gases from escaping, used as an alternative to conventional venting.

Hydrostatic Test

ID: 833 Level 2

A pressure test using water to verify the integrity and leak-tightness of piping systems.

Boiler

ID: 834 Level 2

A closed vessel that heats water or produces steam for heating or industrial processes.

Heat Exchanger

ID: 835 Level 2

A device that transfers heat from one fluid to another without mixing them, commonly used in hydronic heating systems.

Radiant Heating

ID: 836 Level 2

A heating system that warms objects and surfaces directly through infrared radiation rather than heating the air.

Zone Valve

ID: 837 Level 2

A valve that controls water flow to specific heating zones in a hydronic system, allowing independent temperature control.

Backwater Valve

ID: 838 Level 3

A valve installed in a drainage system that prevents sewage from flowing back into a building during sewer surcharge conditions.

Pitch

ID: 839 Level 1

The slope or grade of a pipe, typically measured in inches per foot, required for proper drainage flow.

Invert

ID: 840 Level 3

The lowest point on the inside of a horizontal pipe, used as a reference for establishing proper drainage slope.
